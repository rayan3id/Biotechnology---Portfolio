# Biological Functions of BRCA1

BRCA1 performs several essential cellular functions:

### DNA Repair

BRCA1 is a key component of the homologous recombination repair pathway, which repairs double-strand DNA breaks.

### Genome Stability

The protein helps maintain chromosome integrity and prevents the accumulation of harmful mutations.

### Cell Cycle Regulation

BRCA1 participates in checkpoint control mechanisms that stop cell division when DNA damage is detected.

### Tumor Suppression

BRCA1 acts as a tumor suppressor by preventing uncontrolled cellular proliferation.

### Ubiquitination Activity

BRCA1 functions as an E3 ubiquitin ligase through interaction with BARD1, regulating protein stability and cellular responses to DNA damage.

# Clinical Significance

Mutations in BRCA1 significantly increase the risk of hereditary cancers.

### Associated Diseases

* Hereditary Breast Cancer
* Hereditary Ovarian Cancer
* Pancreatic Cancer
* Familial Cancer Syndromes

Studies indicate that BRCA1 mutations account for a large proportion of inherited breast and ovarian cancer cases worldwide.
